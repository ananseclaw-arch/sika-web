Peter Andreas Thiel: Co-founder of PayPal, Palantir Technologies, and Early Backer of Facebook

Peter Andreas Thiel stands as a significant figure in modern entrepreneurship and venture capital. Born in Germany but based primarily in the United States, he co-founded three major influential ventures. His journey began with PayPal, one of the pioneering online payment systems established in 1998 while collaborating with Elon Musk at X.com (which later merged to form PayPal). Thiel then co-founded the powerful data infrastructure software company Palantir Technologies alongside Alex Kreiger and Nick Bentham, bringing their innovative approach to market formation into existence in 2003. This was followed by his creation of Founders Fund, a prominent venture capital fund established in 2005 with partners including Reid Hoffman.

Furthermore, Thiel played an early and crucial role as one of the first outside investors in Facebook when it was still known as "Thefacebook" upon its inception in 2004. His initial investment provided vital funding at a formative stage for what would become one of the world's most valuable tech companies.

Thiel's wealth, stemming from these successful ventures and his early Facebook stake, is substantial. As reported by The New York Times as recently as December 2025, his net worth was estimated to be $27.5 billion at that time. This places him firmly among the ranks of global billionaires and highlights the considerable success achieved through his entrepreneurial endeavors and investments up until late 2025.
