Laurel, Maryland

Laurel is an incorporated city situated within Prince George's County, Maryland, part of the United States. Its location places it between two major metropolitan areas: Washington and Baltimore.

Strategically positioned on the banks of the Patuxent River, this geography offers a unique setting for one of the region's oldest municipalities. While formally recognized as part of the broader definition of the Washington Metropolitan Area, Laurel itself maintains its distinct identity.

The city's population is documented at approximately 30,060 people based on recent census data, reflecting its current size within Prince George's County. Historically significant, Laurel was founded during the early years of the 19th century and developed primarily as a mill town during that time; this industrial foundation shaped its initial growth patterns.

Over subsequent periods, local industry continued to expand, contributing to the city's development. Following the arrival of the Baltimore and Ohio Railroad in 1835, Laurel experienced further transformation, becoming an early commuter town for residents working in both Washington and Baltimore. This period marked a shift towards residential character as more people sought housing near their workplaces.

Today, Laurel is largely defined by its residential nature. The city actively preserves its historical heritage through the maintenance of a historic district, which centers on Main Street and encompasses many buildings from its earlier periods.
