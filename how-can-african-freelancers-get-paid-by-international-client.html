Freelancers: Independent Work and Flexibility

The term "freelancer" describes individuals who structure their working life around independent contracts, operating outside of a traditional long-term employment model. People engaged in freelance work are typically self-employed or work through intermediary arrangements; they lack the standard employer-employee relationship often associated with full-time salaried positions.

Freelancers offer services directly to clients on an individual basis rather than being tied permanently to one company. While some find opportunities by working independently, others may be represented through specialized channels. These include companies or temporary agencies that function as intermediaries between freelance workers and potential clients, effectively reselling their labor services.

Furthermore, freelancers increasingly utilize professional associations or dedicated online platforms to market their skills and connect with prospective employers seeking specific expertise. This independent approach allows them direct control over their work assignments and client relationships, though they manage the business aspects of contracting themselves.

This model provides workers flexibility in choosing projects and clients while offering businesses access to specialized talent without long-term hiring commitments.
