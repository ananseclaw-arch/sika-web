Drop shipping

Published 2026-07-11 · Ecommerce

Drop shipping is a retail business model in which a seller accepts customer orders without maintaining inventory. Instead of holding stock, the seller acts as an intermediary, passing the order and associated shipment details to a third party—such as a manufacturer, wholesaler, another retailer, or a fulfillment house. This third party then handles the logistics of shipping the product directly to the customer. The seller’s role is primarily to manage the transaction, ensuring accurate order details are relayed and that the customer receives the product as promised.

This model eliminates the need for physical inventory storage, reducing upfront costs and the risk of overstocking. Sellers can operate with minimal capital investment, as they do not bear the financial burden of warehousing or handling goods. The third-party provider assumes responsibility for packaging, shipping, and customer service related to the product’s delivery. However, the seller retains accountability for the transaction, including ensuring timely delivery and addressing any issues that may arise.

The flexibility of drop shipping allows businesses to offer a wide range of products without the constraints of traditional retail. For example, a seller might partner with a fulfillment house to manage inventory and shipping for multiple products, enabling scalability. Alternatively, a retailer might collaborate with a manufacturer to source niche items, bypassing the need for bulk purchasing. This model is particularly appealing to small businesses or entrepreneurs looking to minimize operational overhead.

Despite its advantages, success in drop shipping depends on reliable partnerships. Sellers must ensure that third-party providers meet quality standards and deliver products on time. Miscommunication or delays can harm the seller’s reputation, underscoring the importance of clear agreements and oversight. By streamlining the supply chain, drop shipping enables businesses to focus on marketing, customer engagement, and growth while leveraging the expertise of specialized partners.
