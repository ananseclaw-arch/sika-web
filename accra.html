Accra

Published 2026-07-11 · Ghana Cities

Accra, the capital and largest city of Ghana, is situated on the southern coast, along the Gulf of Guinea, which borders the Atlantic Ocean. As a key urban center, it serves as the political, economic, and cultural hub of the country. The city's administrative boundaries are divided into two primary regions: the Accra Metropolitan District and the broader Greater Accra Region. According to the 2021 census, the Accra Metropolitan District, covering 20.4 square kilometers, had a population of 284,124. In contrast, the larger Greater Accra Region, spanning 3,245 square kilometers, was home to 5.46 million people, underscoring the city's role as a major population center.

The name "Accra" historically refers to the territory of the Accra Metropolitan District as it existed before 2008, when it encompassed 199.4 square kilometers. This area, reportedly, housed 1,782,150 inhabitants at the 2021 census. Over time, this territory was split into 13 local government districts, comprising 12 independent municipal districts and the reduced Accra Metropolitan District. The latter, now the only district within the capital granted city status, is distinguished as the "City of Accra." This distinction highlights the administrative and geographical separation between the core city and the surrounding regions.

The split in 2008 redefined the boundaries of the metropolitan area, allowing for more localized governance. While the broader Greater Accra Region includes suburban and rural areas, the City of Accra remains the administrative heart of Ghana. This structure reflects the city's evolution from a centralized administrative zone to a more decentralized system, balancing urban development with regional governance. The population dynamics and territorial divisions underscore Accra's significance as both a city and a regional capital, shaping its identity and role in Ghana's socio-economic landscape.
